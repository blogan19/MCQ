Constipation/Diarrhoea


It is important as a pharmacist that you are aware of drugs which may commonly cause constipation or diarrhoea
Constipation
Aluminium containing antacids
Iron preparations
Calcium supplements
Antispasmodics (dicycloverine, mebeverine, peppermint oil)
Colestyramine
Calcium channel blockers(e.g. propranolol, verapamil)
Sedating antihistamines (e.g. chlorphenamine, promethazine)
Opioid analgesics (e.g.codeine, dihydrocodeine, morphine, tramadol, oxcodone)
Tricyclic antidepressants e.g. amitriptyline, dosulepin, imipramine
Antipsychotics (e.g. amisulpride, chlorpromazine, clozapine)
Antiparkinson drugs (e.g. entacapone, pramipexole, procyclidine, selegiline)
Antiepileptic drugs (e.g. carbamazepine, oxcarbazepine, phenytoin, pregabalin)
Alpha blockers (e.g. prazosin, tamsulosin)
Diarrhoea
Magnesium containing antacids
H2-receptor antagonists
Proton pump inhibitors
Aminosalicylates e.g. mesalazine, olsalazine, sulfasalazine,
Misoprostol
Orlistat (steatorrhea)
Antibiotics particularly broad spectrum which increase the risk of clostridium difficile
Sulphonylureas
Metformin
Acarbose
Levothyroxine
Cytotoxic drugs such as methotrexate
Colchicine
Leflunomide
