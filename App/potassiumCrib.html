Potassium


It is important as a pharmacist that you are aware of drugs which may commonly affect potassium levels
Hyperkalaemia
Potassium-sparing diuretics and aldosterone antagonists such as spironolactone and eplerenone
Angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors (ACE inhibitors)
Angiotensin receptor antagonists
Aliskiren
Low-molecular weight heparin
Trimethoprim and co-trimoxazole
Ciclosporin
Tacrolimus
Potassium supplements or salts
Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)
Hypokalaemia
Laxatives
All type of Diuretics
Corticosteroids
High dose insulin (can be used as treatment for hyperkalaemia)
High dose beta agonists such as nebulised salbutamol (can be used as treatment for hyperkalaemia)
Theophylline
Gentamicin
Amphotericin
Echinocandin antifungals
Sodium bicarbonate
